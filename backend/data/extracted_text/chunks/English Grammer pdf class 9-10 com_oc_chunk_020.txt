6 English Grammar and Composition
B Plurals are usually formed by adding s, es or ies to the singular form. Look
at the following examples.
(i) Singular + s = plural
boy boys
girl girls
dog dogs
page pages
monkey monkeys
Note: Most nouns are made plural by adding an s to the singular form.
(ii) Singular + es = plural
bus buses
kiss kisses
dish dishes
watch watches
box boxes
Note: Plurals are formed by adding es when the singular form ends in s, ss, sh,
ch and x.
(iii) Singular + ies = plural
baby babies
lady ladies
story stories
duty duties
fly flies
Note: Singular nouns ending in a consonant + y form their plurals by dropping
y and adding ies.
